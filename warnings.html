A SIGNAL, NOT A SCARE

The Warnings of God

Why a loving God puts up warning signs — and how we keep our footing while we wait for the final chapter.

— 1 Corinthians 10:11 (NIV)

SECTION 01

How to read a warning in the Bible

Think about a road sign that says “Bridge Out Ahead.” The sign is not there to ruin your trip or scare you. It is there because someone who can see the danger up ahead loves you enough to tell you before it is too late. That is exactly what a biblical warning is — God can see what is coming, and He tells us in time.

Before we list the warnings, here is a simple 5-step way to understand any warning you find in Scripture. Use these same five questions every time.

STEP 1 — WHO

Who was it spoken to?

A whole nation? A single king? A young church under pressure? The first audience shapes the meaning.

STEP 2 — WHAT

What is the danger?

Name the exact thing God says to avoid: pride, idol worship, drifting away, false teachers.

STEP 3 — WHY

Why does it matter?

Every warning protects something good — life, relationship with God, the safety of others.

STEP 4 — THEN

What happened next?

Did people listen and get spared, or ignore it and get hurt? History is the proof.

STEP 5 — NOW

What does it mean for me today?

A warning is only useful if it changes how you live this week. This is the whole point.

SECTION 02

Why does God warn people at all?

Here is the big idea of this whole study: a warning is an act of love. If a parent sees their child reaching for a hot stove, the loving thing is to shout “Stop!” God's warnings work the same way. They come from care, not cruelty. Below are the four main reasons God warns.

The warnings of Jesus

Jesus warned more about some dangers than almost anyone else in the Bible — and He did it out of love. He warned crowds, religious leaders, and His own closest friends. Tap a card for the full background.

SECTION 05

The apostles' warnings to the early church

After Jesus returned to heaven, His followers (the apostles) wrote letters to brand-new churches. These churches faced pressure, fake teachers, and the temptation to give up. The warnings in these letters are some of the most direct in the whole Bible.

SECTION 07

Warnings about the final chapter — Jesus' return

The Bible says history is heading somewhere: Jesus promised He will come back. Almost every warning about His return carries the same one-word command — “Watch.” Not anxious watching, but ready, awake, faithful living. Tap a card for the background.

SECTION 08

How to stand, stay focused, and respond well

Knowing the warnings is not enough — we have to respond. The Bible does not leave us guessing. Here are seven practical habits, each tied to a verse, that help a believer stay steady while we wait for the final chapter.

1 · Stay awake and alert

Live ready, not asleep. Matthew 24:42

2 · Stay in God's Word

It is a lamp for your feet. Psalm 119:105

3 · Pray and stay close to God

Watch and pray. Mark 14:38

4 · Stay connected to others

Encourage one another daily. Hebrews 3:13

5 · Test what you are taught

Don't believe every teacher. 1 John 4:1

6 · Stand firm under pressure

Put on the full armor of God. Ephesians 6:11

7 · Keep your eyes on Jesus

Fix your eyes on Jesus, the author and finisher of faith. Hebrews 12:1–2

SECTION 09

The Sermon

“The Sign That Saves You”

Main text: 1 Corinthians 10:11–13 · Supporting text: Matthew 24:42–44

Opening. Imagine driving down a highway at night when your headlights catch a bright orange sign: “Danger — Bridge Out.” You have two choices. You can get annoyed that someone interrupted your drive, or you can be grateful that someone cared enough to put it there. Tonight I want to show you that every warning in the Bible is that orange sign — and the God who put it there is not trying to spoil your trip. He is trying to save your life.

Point 1 — Warnings are written in love, for us.

Paul tells the church in Corinth that the failures of Israel “were written down as warnings for us, on whom the culmination of the ages has come” (1 Corinthians 10:11). Notice two things. First, the warnings were written down — God made sure we would still have them. Second, they are for us. The stories of people who stumbled long ago are not there to make us feel superior; they are there to keep us from repeating the same mistakes.

Point 2 — Warnings expose where we feel too safe.

The very next verse is the punch: “So, if you think you are standing firm, be careful that you don't fall” (1 Corinthians 10:12). The most dangerous place to stand is on top of confidence that has stopped depending on God. Warnings find the spot where we have quietly told ourselves, “That could never be me.” That is usually the exact spot where we are weakest.

Point 3 — Warnings always come with a way out.

And here is the mercy. God never warns us and then abandons us. Verse 13 promises that He “will also provide a way out so that you can endure it.” A warning is never the end of the conversation — it is God opening a door before the trouble arrives. The bridge may be out, but He has already marked the detour.

Point 4 — We live in the waiting, so we live awake.

We are people who live between two great events: Jesus has already come once, and He has promised to come again. Jesus said, “Therefore keep watch, because you do not know on what day your Lord will come” (Matthew 24:42). Watching does not mean staring at the sky in fear. It means living each ordinary day as if it matters — because it does. Staying in His Word, praying, loving people, refusing the things He warned us about.

Closing — Don't drive past the sign.

So what will you do with the orange sign? You can speed past it, or you can slow down, trust the One who placed it, and take the way out He has already provided. The warnings of God are not the shout of an angry stranger. They are the voice of a Father on the other side of the danger, calling, “This way home.” Let's not ignore the sign that was put there to save us.
