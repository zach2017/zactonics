How to Use Both Tools Safely

Safe Use Checklist

Practical steps to protect your data whether you're an individual or managing a team.

01
Use OAuth — never share passwords
Both tools connect via OAuth 2.0 to Google or Outlook. Always authenticate through the official sign-in flow. If any screen asks for your calendar password directly, stop and report it.
02
Enable MFA on your Google / Outlook account
Your calendar access token is only as secure as the account it comes from. Enable multi-factor authentication on Google or Microsoft to prevent account takeover at the source.
03
Audit connected app permissions
Review which permissions Motion and Reclaim have. In Google: myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps. Revoke access for any integration you no longer use.
04
Keep meeting titles generic for sensitive topics
Both tools read event titles and descriptions to schedule intelligently. For highly confidential meetings (legal, HR, M&A), use generic titles like "Internal Review" rather than specifics.
05
Review team data access policies
If using team features in Motion (workload view) or Reclaim (people analytics), confirm with your team what calendar data is visible to managers or colleagues. Check each tool's team settings.
06
Enterprise? Request a DPA
For regulated industries (healthcare, legal, finance), request a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) before deployment. Both tools support DPAs for enterprise/business contracts covering GDPR obligations.
07
Understand data deletion rights
Under GDPR and CCPA you have the right to request deletion of your data. Both tools have account deletion flows — for full data removal, use the in-app deletion and follow up if needed via their privacy contacts.
08
Keep apps and integrations updated
Use the latest version of both tools. Security patches are rolled out continuously — especially relevant for the Motion desktop/mobile app and the Reclaim Chrome extension if installed.
09
Monitor for phishing impersonation
Legitimate emails from Motion come from @usemotion.com and Reclaim from @reclaim.ai. Be cautious of any unexpected email asking you to re-authenticate or enter credentials outside the app.
